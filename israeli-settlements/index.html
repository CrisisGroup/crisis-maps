About This Map

Plan E1 is a settlement construction project in an area east of Jerusalem inside the West Bank, whose construction was approved in August 2025, after being frozen for decades due to U.S. objections.

Israeli bypass roads are the ones which only Israeli-licensed vehicles (including vehicles belonging to Palestinians with Jerusalem residence permits) are allowed to drive.

Planned bypass or “fabric of life” roads for Palestinians are solely for West Bank Palestinians. On 29 March, 2025, the Israeli cabinet authorised a bypass road designated exclusively for West Bank Palestinians to allow seamless connection of Israel’s own road system in occupied territory and set the stage for the E1 settlement. Both the road and the east-west extension of Israeli settlements will in effect cleave the West Bank in two, making it easy for Israel to block Palestinians’ travel between north and south, and further isolate both parts of the West Bank from the large Palestinian population of East Jerusalem.

Settlements in the West Bank that were officially established by the government (East Jerusalem excluded).

Outposts are settlements that were established since the 1990's without government approval and are considered illegal according to Israeli law.
